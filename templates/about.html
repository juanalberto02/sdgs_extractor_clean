ORGANIZATION PROFILE

The Institute for Innovation, Journal Development, Publishing, and Intellectual Property Rights was legalized in 2020 with Decree Number 868/UN3/2020. The Institute for Innovation, Journal Development, Publishing, and Intellectual Property Rights oversees three service units, namely publishing and journals, innovation and IPR, and publishing/Airlangga University Press.

Publications and Journals

The Publication and Journal Division is another name for the Center for Scientific Journal and Publication Development (PPJPI) which is a unit in the coordination of Division 3 (Research and Cooperation). This unit began with the formation of the Scientific Journal Development Team (TPJI) in 2005 which played a role in developing scientific journals according to quality standards. Because its current function is in assisting publications, since 2015 its name has changed to the Center for Scientific Journal and Publication Development (PPJPI). In 2020, PPJPI merged into one Publishing division at the Institute for Innovation, Journal Development, Publishing, and Intellectual Property Rights (LIPJPHKI). 

The main task of this service is to conduct data collection, monitoring, coordination, and assistance related to scientific journals. In addition to these duties, this field also provides assistance for international publication of scientific manuscripts by the academic community of Airlangga University from the submission stage to the publication stage, coordinates the provision of scientific publication incentives in Scopus/ISI Thomson Indexed International Journals (IPKI) and also carries out verification of h-index and related citations.

Innovation and IPR

Intellectual Property Rights has been part of the Business Development and Incubation Institute (LPBI) since 2017. Starting in 2020, Intellectual Property Rights has become one of the service units under the auspices of the Institute for Innovation, Journal Development, Publishing and Intellectual Property Rights (LIPJPHKI). This service unit is tasked with facilitating the acquisition of patents, copyrights, and trademarks.
